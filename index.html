About

Hello, my name is Lina Simavičiūtė (Alba).

A human being, doctor, caregiver, therapist, artist, and lifelong student of human experience.

Born in Lithuania in 1975, I come from a family with three generations dedicated to health and care. I graduated from Kaunas University of Medicine and later qualified as a General Practitioner in Lithuania. My academic journey also includes Master’s degrees in Public Health, International Relations and Diplomacy, and Law and Administration.

Alongside conventional medicine, I have studied hypnotherapy, Reiki, massage, homeopathy, coaching approaches, healing traditions, and holistic wellbeing practices.

For many years I have supported people through illness, recovery, disability, mobility challenges, loneliness, significant life events, grief, ageing, and end-of-life transitions. Since 2022, I have been supporting people across the United Kingdom through companionship, wellbeing support, recovery, life transitions, ageing, grief, and end-of-life journeys.

The foundation of my work is a combination of professional knowledge and human presence.

A Personal Note

Hello, my name is Lina.

I provide companionship and wellbeing support for people who would like a bit more company, practical help, or simply another person around during the day.

Sometimes that means having a chat over a cup of tea, going for a walk, helping with shopping, attending appointments, reading together, or just being there so that someone feels less alone and more confident in daily life.

My approach is simple: professional knowledge and human presence. I believe that people do better when they feel seen, heard, respected, and free to make their own choices. Small moments of connection, comfort, and understanding can make a meaningful difference to wellbeing, confidence, and happiness.

Every person is different, so I always start with a conversation to see what would be most helpful.

My Philosophy

The ALBA Wellbeing Approach

Through medicine, caregiving, art, study, and life experience, I developed what I call the ALBA Wellbeing Approach. At its heart is a simple belief:

Every human being deserves dignity, respect, compassion, understanding, happiness, and freedom of choice.

I believe that meaningful change begins when people feel:

01

Safe enough to be honest.

02

Comfortable enough to relax.

03

Accepted enough to be themselves.

04

Trusted enough to make their own choices.

05

Supported enough to move forward.

My role is not to tell people who they should be.

My role is to help create conditions where people can discover their own answers, reconnect with themselves, and make their own right choices.

Transparent Happiness

Happiness is not perfection

Happiness can exist alongside challenge, grief, uncertainty, recovery, change, and growth.

Transparent happiness begins when we honestly accept ourselves and our experiences with openness, compassion, and awareness.

The purpose of my work is to help create conditions where wellbeing, peace, resilience, authenticity, happiness, and quality of life can naturally grow.

For me, wellbeing includes

  • Body
  • Mind
  • Emotions
  • Relationships
  • Values
  • Meaning
  • Soul
  • Hope
  • Human connection

Important Information

My services are designed to support wellbeing and quality of life. They do not replace medical diagnosis, treatment, nursing care, psychological therapy, emergency services, or advice from your GP or healthcare team.

All services are provided with informed consent, respect, confidentiality, and acceptance of each person’s individual beliefs, values, and choices.

Confidentiality

Respect, trust, and confidentiality are important foundations of my work. Information shared during conversations and sessions will be treated with care, respect, and confidentiality.

Personal information will not be shared with third parties without your permission, except where required by law, safeguarding responsibilities, or where there is a serious concern regarding the safety of yourself or another person.
