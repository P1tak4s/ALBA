About

Hello, my name is Lina — known to many as Alba.

A professional live-in carer and wellbeing companion. Professional knowledge with genuine human presence — together, creating a happy we.

Book a free consultation See how I can help

Originally from Lithuania, I have permanent permission to live and work in the UK.

I am medically trained, professionally qualified, self-employed, and have five years of care experience in the UK. I hold a full driving licence and am happy to travel for the right opportunity.

I can offer reliable, long-term availability and have no plans for extended holidays. I prefer a stable, mutually agreed routine rather than constantly changing shifts, as this brings consistency and security for both the person receiving care and their family — while I remain flexible and adaptable to the person’s changing needs.

I live with an open heart and a deep respect for life, people, nature and animals. I believe every person deserves kindness, dignity, understanding and genuine human connection. Living together is a special relationship built on trust, respect, understanding and shared everyday life.

My philosophy

I believe every day matters

Life is not lived by protocol alone.

Professional knowledge, rehabilitation plans and best practice provide the foundation. Genuine care also requires flexibility, understanding and human presence.

Every person is unique. Every day brings different needs. My role is to adapt with knowledge, respect, kindness and calm — supporting both the person and their family through everyday life. I believe that to care well for others, I must also care for myself, so I maintain a healthy daily routine and look after my own physical, mental and soulful wellbeing. This helps me bring positive energy, calmness, resilience and consistency to the people I support.

Happiness grows when it is shared.

My approach

The right choice

I believe life is shaped by the choices we make every day.

The right choice is not about being perfect. It is about making thoughtful decisions that support health, dignity, comfort, independence, wellbeing and happiness. Every person has their own personality, values, wishes, family, culture, language, traditions, beliefs and life story. Together we can make the right choices for everyday life — creating routines, adapting to changing needs, supporting rehabilitation, maintaining independence, and enjoying the simple moments that bring comfort and joy.

The right choice is listening before acting. It is respecting rather than replacing independence. It is adapting rather than expecting everyone to fit the same routine. It is combining professional knowledge with genuine human presence.

Happiness grows when we make the right choices together.

How I work

Five principles I return to

Respect

The whole person

For each person’s individuality, dignity, family, culture, traditions, beliefs, values and choices — never a diagnosis or an age.

The right choice

Choose yourself

My role is not to decide for you, but to help you understand the options and choose what feels right.

Flexibility

Meeting you where you are

Every person and situation is different, so support adapts to your needs, wishes, abilities, routines and stage of life.

Independence

Strengthening, not replacing

Wherever possible, support should protect your confidence, autonomy and involvement in your own decisions.

Human connection

A relationship built on trust, consent, confidentiality and mutual respect — sometimes another’s hands, eyes or ears, sometimes simply a calm presence.
